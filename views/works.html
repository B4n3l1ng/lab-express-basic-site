Metallica

Having been credited with writing or co-writing over 80% of Metallica's songs, James Hetfield is considered one of the most hands-on frontman of all time.

In 2009, he was ranked No.8 in Joel McIver's book The 100 Greatest Metal Guitarists and No. 24 by Hit Parader on their list of the 100 Greatest Metal Vocalists of All Time.
In Guitar World's poll, Hetfield was placed as the 19th greatest guitarist of all time. Rolling Stone magazine placed him as the 87th greatest guitarist of all time.

Discography

Studio Albums

Kill'Em All

First studio album, released in 1983, peaked No 66 in North American charts.

Ride the Lightning

Second studio album, released in 1984, peaked No 48 in North American charts, with 4.3 million sales worldwide.

Master of Puppets

Third studio album, released in 1986, peaked No. 29 in North American charts, with 4.5 million sales worldwide.

...And Justice for All

Fourth studio album, released in 1988, first album with their second bass player, Jason Newstad, after the tragic passing of founding member Cliff Burton. Peaked at number 6 in North American charts, with 5.3 million sales worldwide.

Metallica or Black Album

Fifth studio album, released in 1991, peaked at number 1 worldwide, with 17.3 million sales worldwide.

Load

Sixth studio album, released in 1996, peaked at number 1 worldwide, with 5 million sales worlwide.

Reload

Seventh studio album, released in 1997, peaked at number 1 in North American charts, with 4 millions sales worlwide.

St.Anger

Eight studio album, released in 2003. First studio album with new bass player, Robert Trujillo, after the departure of Jason Newstead. Peaked at number 1 Worldwide, with 2 million sales worlwide.

Death Magnetic

Ninth studio album, released in 2008, peaked at number 1 worldwide with 2 million sales in the US alone.

Hardwired... to Self-Destruct

Tenth studio album, released in 2016, first and only album under their own label, Blackend, peaked at number 1 worlwide with 1 million sales in the US alone.
