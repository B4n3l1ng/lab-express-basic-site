James Alan Hetfield

Born August 13, 1963, James Hetfield is an American musician and songwriter.
Lead vocalist, rythm guitarist, co-founder and main songwriter of heavy metal band Metallica. Mainly known for his intricate rhythm playing, although he occasionally performs lead guitarist duties, both live and in recordings.
Hetfield co-founded Metallica in October 1981, after answering an ad by drummer Lars Ulrich. Metallica has won 9 Grammy Awards and released ten studio albums, three live albums, four extended plays and 24 singles.

Personal Life

Hetfield married Francesca Tomasi on August 17, 1997, having three children together. They lived together in Vail, Colorado for 25 years, before Hetfield filed for divorce in 2022.
Hetfield's son, Castor is also a musician for the band Bastardane, based in Svannah, Georgia.
Hetfield has logn struggled with addiction problems. In 2001, he entered a rebah programm (documented in the film Metallica: Some Kind of Monster), and began maintaining total abstinence from alcohol. In a 2010 intervied with So What!, the official magazine of Metallica's fan club, Helfield stated that he is a "reborn straight edge". However, in 2019, Hetfield once again entered rehab, forcing Metallica to cancel their Australia/New Zealand leg of the WorldWired Tour.
